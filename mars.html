Landmarks

Mars Record Breakers

Mars holds several solar system records for extraordinary geological features.

🌋

Olympus Mons

~21.9 km tall

The tallest volcano in the solar system — nearly 3× the height of Mount Everest.

🏜️

Valles Marineris

~4,000 km long

The longest canyon in the solar system — as wide as the United States.

❄️

Polar Ice Caps

Both poles

Water ice and CO₂ (dry ice) caps that grow and shrink with the Martian seasons.

🌪️

Dust Storms

Planet-wide

Global dust storms can last for months, blanketing the entire planet in reddish haze.

🌍

Size vs Earth

Half of Earth

About half Earth's diameter, yet similar land area because Mars has no oceans.

🔴

Why Red?

Iron Oxide

Rust (iron oxide) coats the surface dust and rocks, giving Mars its iconic red colour.

Mars has a very thin atmosphere — about 1% the pressure of Earth's — and is mostly carbon dioxide. With no ozone layer, the surface is bathed in high UV radiation. Temperature swings are extreme. Liquid water cannot exist at the surface due to the low atmospheric pressure.

Phobos

  • Diameter22.2 km
  • Orbital Period7.65 hours
  • Distance from Mars9,376 km
  • FateWill crash in ~50M years

Deimos

  • Diameter12.4 km
  • Orbital Period30.3 hours
  • Distance from Mars23,460 km
  • FateSlowly drifting away

Mars has two small, irregularly shaped moons discovered in 1877. They are thought to be captured asteroids. Phobos orbits so close it rises and sets three times a day; tidal forces will cause it to crash into Mars or break apart in ~50 million years.

Challenges

  • RadiationNo magnetic field
  • TemperatureExtremely cold average
  • AtmosphereThin, mostly CO₂
  • Liquid waterCannot exist at surface

Mars is considered one of the most promising candidates for extraterrestrial life. Billions of years ago it had liquid water; NASA's Curiosity rover detected organic molecules and seasonal methane spikes. Whether life ever existed — or survives today in protected niches — remains one of science's greatest open questions.

History

Mars Missions Timeline

1965

Mariner 4 — First Flyby

First successful flyby of Mars, returning 21 photographs — the first close-up images of another planet.

1976

Viking Landers

Viking 1 and 2 were the first spacecraft to land on Mars and conduct experiments to search for signs of life.

1997

Pathfinder & Sojourner

The first Mars rover, Sojourner, operated for 83 days and analysed rocks and soil on the surface.

2004

Spirit & Opportunity

Found strong evidence that liquid water once existed on Mars. Opportunity operated for over 14 years.

2012

Curiosity Rover

Still active — detected organic molecules and confirmed ancient Mars was habitable.

2021

Perseverance & Ingenuity

Perseverance collects rock samples for return to Earth. Ingenuity became the first powered aircraft to fly on another planet.

2030s

First Human Mission (Planned)

NASA and SpaceX plan crewed Mars missions — humanity's first steps on another planet.

Mars FAQ

Living on Mars would require pressurised habitats, oxygen generation, radiation shielding, and food production. The thin atmosphere, extreme cold, and high radiation make the surface inhospitable without advanced technology. However, Mars's day length (24h 37m) and axial tilt (25°) are similar to Earth's, making some aspects easier for adaptation.
A typical mission using Hohmann transfer orbits takes about 7–9 months. The distance varies from 56 million km to 401 million km depending on orbital positions. SpaceX's Starship aims to significantly reduce travel time with more powerful propulsion.
Yes! Mars has significant water primarily as ice in its polar caps and in the subsurface. In 2018, scientists detected what appears to be a subsurface liquid water lake beneath the south polar ice cap using ground-penetrating radar. Surface water cannot exist stably due to the thin atmosphere.
Mars gets its reddish colour from iron oxide — essentially rust — that coats the surface rocks and dust. This colour is visible even to the naked eye, which is why ancient astronomers named it after the Roman god of war.
