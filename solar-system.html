The Planets

Meet the Eight Planets

From the scorching rocky worlds of the inner solar system to the giant gas worlds of the outer reaches.

⚫

Mercury

The smallest planet and closest to the Sun. Extreme temperature swings from -180°C to 430°C with no atmosphere.

88 Earth days per orbit
🌕

Venus

The hottest planet (462°C average) despite not being closest to the Sun. Thick CO₂ atmosphere with sulfuric acid clouds.

225 Earth days per orbit
🌍

Earth

Our home — the only known planet with life. Protected by a magnetic field, liquid water covers 71% of its surface.

365.25 Earth days per orbit
🔴

Mars

The Red Planet has the solar system's tallest volcano (Olympus Mons) and longest canyon (Valles Marineris).

687 Earth days per orbit
🟠

Jupiter

The largest planet — over 1,300 Earths could fit inside. The Great Red Spot is a 400-year-old storm system.

11.9 Earth years per orbit
🪐

Saturn

Famous for its stunning ring system made of ice and rock. So light it could float on water.

29.5 Earth years per orbit
🔵

Uranus

Rotates on its side with an axial tilt of 98°. Its blue-green color comes from methane in its atmosphere.

84 Earth years per orbit
🌀

Neptune

The windiest planet with speeds up to 2,100 km/h. Takes 165 years to orbit the Sun once.

165 Earth years per orbit

More to Explore

Beyond the Planets

The solar system contains much more than just the eight planets.

🌑

The Moon

Earth's only natural satellite, 384,400 km away. Controls tides and stabilizes Earth's axial tilt.

☄️

Asteroid Belt

Located between Mars and Jupiter, containing millions of rocky objects, remnants from the solar system's formation.

🌠

Kuiper Belt

Beyond Neptune lies the Kuiper Belt — a vast region of icy bodies including dwarf planet Pluto.

💫

Oort Cloud

The most distant region of the solar system — a spherical shell of icy bodies that may extend 2 light-years from the Sun.

🌟

Dwarf Planets

Pluto, Eris, Ceres, Makemake, and Haumea — five officially recognized dwarf planets so far discovered.

🚀

Space Probes

Voyager 1, launched in 1977, is now in interstellar space — humanity's farthest-reaching exploration.
